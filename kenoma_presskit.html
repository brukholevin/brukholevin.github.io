About Kenoma

Kenoma: Action Without Action is a 2D-sidescrolling graphic adventure game with
two-worlds-at-once traversal, much like that level in Titanfall 2 but without time travel.
It is focused primarily on narrative and an eclectic cast of characters, and the story revolves around your choice of which of the two worlds to destroy. The typical graphic adventure structures are in place,
with an added element of 'diorama' setpieces, where players interact more directly with the environment. For example, brushing bones away from a wall carving so that it
can be properly read.

English and Simplified Chinese languages supported.

Rundown

  • 2D-sidescrolling graphic adventure
  • Core mechanic: switching between two worlds on the fly
  • One world must be saved and the other destroyed. Which is which is decided by the player
  • Locations are visually diverse and filled with real symbology
  • Interact with strongly-written characters, many of which have their own goals and paths
  • Major themes of existentialism, mortality, religion, and power
  • Classic adventure game puzzles and exploration are accented by small 'diorama' setpieces
  • Project of solo developer "Jack Levin"

About BrukhoLevin

BrukhoLevin consists of owner/proprietor Jack Levin, and no one else. I've been an artist in various mediums for five years; including music, visual art, writing, and game development. I'm classically trained in programming, and have been doing that for seven years. I am very passionate about games as an artistic medium, and hope to provide novel and fun experiences while keeping things relatively highbrow.
