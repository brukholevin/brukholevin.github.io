Snow:
It is cold. Too cold. At least too cold to be wearing nothing from the waist up. I struggle on regardless. They will return if I do not seem strong enough to continue. I know that I do not look strong enough moving as I am now, limping and stumbling. It does not matter. There is no choice in this.
The lone moon looks down on me. You cannot call it shining. I could scarcely tell you where it is without how it blocks the stars. Pity that it sees the cold and empty void of space instead of the cold and empty void of Earth I am in. Even the skies shun this place.
I mindlessly pass small trees, victims of winter covered in sheets of pure white. A blanket of cold. Ironic. Even the old symbols of strength and stability bend to this force. Why shouldn't I?
The evergreens remain nonplussed.
